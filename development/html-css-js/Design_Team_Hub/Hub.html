What is User Testing?

User testing covers a variety of methods and methodologies but essentially it's about gathering insights from our users that we can use to improve our product and also test directly our concepts and designs.

Broadly speaking it can be divided into two main categories: moderated and unmoderated.

Moderated is where one or more of the design team would be present to either ask questions directly, supervise and direct the testing or observe and take notes. Typically this would be an interview, testing with an interactive prototype, or observing the user carrying-out a typical task using the software. The general rule of thumb is that these tests would take 40mins-1hr to complete and importantly these are conducted with a single participant so that participants cannot see/hear each other and influence each other's actions.

Unmoderated would be a test that the user can conduct in their own time. Typically they would have a link to an online test which could be a survey, card sorting, first click test. These are shorter as a rule of thumb and should take no more than 30mins but typically would be about 10-15mins.

Why do we want to test with users?

Without gathering feedback and gaining insights from our clients we can only guess what their needs are and whether our platform is meeting them. Unlike a lot of larger competitors of ours, we give our clients a direct line to the Product team to provide feedback. “Your feedback is important to us” is not just an empty catchphrase at SMC, it's what informs and powers the design process.

By taking part in user testing you are helping to shape the future and vision of SMC. We want to hear your worst frustrations and turn them into positives by using those insights to improve the platform and develop new features.

How can you get involved?

From time to time, you will receive communications via your CSMs about what features are being developed that we are actively doing testing on. They will then put you in contact with the Product Design Team who will take it from there.

You can also refer to this page and the section below where we will maintain a list of any active/ongoing tests that you can participate in.

Alternatively, if you actively want to participate in doing some User Testing right now then let your CSM know and they will arrange to put you in touch with the Product Design Team :)
